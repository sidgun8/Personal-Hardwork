## Diffusion
Diffusion models generate high-quality images by iteratively denoising a noise-corrupted signal.  The
Diffusion Transformer (DiT) replaces the traditional CNN-based U-Net backbone with a scalable
Transformer,  enabling better image fidelity at higher resolutions.  While these models excel glob-
ally,  complex  or  fine-grained  regions  often  demand  computationally  expensive  cyclic  refinement
(re-injecting noise and re-denoising).  Fixed spatial heuristics like Region-Aware Sampling (RAS)
apply this refinement uniformly, ignoring per-image semantics.  To remedy this, you will implement
Regionally-Adaptive  Cyclic  Diffusion  (RACD): train a lightweight “Difficulty Predictor” that spa-
tially  identifies  which  latent  regions  need  cyclic  refinement,  maximising  fidelity  while  minimising
compute.
Dataset:  Train on the landscape dataset (no class conditioning).
## 1.  Baseline Global Cyclic Refinement
Cyclic refinement is a technique that improves sample quality by re-injecting a small amount
of noise into already-denoised latents and running additional denoising steps.  This acts as a
“polish” pass, correcting residual errors at the cost of extra compute.
(a)  Implement a global cyclic refinement step on your baseline DiT trained on the landscape
dataset.  After standard sampling reaches t = 0, inject noise back into the latents up to
t = 200, then denoise back to t = 0.
(b)  Measure average generation time and FID/CMMD. Do you notice a visual improvement?
Explain the compute trade-off.

## Mechanistic Interpretability

but efficient deployment often requires low-bit activation quantisation.  While quantisation reduces
